Obliteration Methods 01

Three
Ways to
Destroy.

no file system pointer removal. no temporary erasure. OBLVN overwrites, encrypts, and verifies at the physical level, then issues legal proof it happened.

01
Binary Obliteration

multi-pass overwrite replaces every magnetic bit. DoD 5220.22-M, Gutmann 35-pass, or NIST 800-88, auto-selected by device type. HDD and magnetic media.

DoD 5220.22-M · Gutmann · NIST 800-88

AES-256-CBC · FIPS 140-2 · SSD / NVMe

overwrite passes, sector verification read-back, crypto seal. every byte confirmed. S.M.A.R.T. snapshot captured. certificate issued and Bitcoin-anchored.

ISO 27001 · HIPAA §164.310 · SOC 2
How It Works 02

Six steps
to zero.

from connection to certificate, every action is logged, verified, and cryptographically sealed. nothing is left to chance.

01
Connect your device

attach a storage device to your machine or upload files directly in the browser. OBLVN detects connected hardware automatically and reads S.M.A.R.T. health data before touching any sector.

Auto-detection · S.M.A.R.T. snapshot
02
Select your method

choose Binary Obliteration, Crypto Erasure, or Full Sanitization. OBLVN recommends the right standard for your device type automatically — DoD for HDDs, NIST for SSDs.

DoD 5220.22-M · NIST 800-88 · Gutmann 35-pass
03
Approval gate (optional)

in organisations with the approval gate enabled, wipe jobs created by operators are held in a pending state until a Team Lead or Org Admin authorises execution. full role separation.

Role-based access · Org Admin · Team Lead · Operator
04
Destruction executes

the wipe runs on the machine with real-time progress streamed to the interface. every pass is tracked. for crypto erasure, the AES-256 key is shown once for the key destruction ceremony, then zeroed from memory using ctypes.memset.

AES-256 · Real-time progress · Key ceremony
05
Verification pass

after overwriting completes, OBLVN runs a sector-by-sector read-back to confirm no recoverable data remains. the result is included in the certificate as a pass or fail.

Read-back verification · Sector-level confirmation
06
Certificate issued and anchored

a tamper-proof PDF certificate is generated with the SHA-256 hash of the wipe, then submitted to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps. the proof exists permanently, even if OBLVN servers go offline.

SHA-256 · OpenTimestamps · Bitcoin permanence
